Shoe Budgeting

Justifying a $600 Pair of Shoes

My Original Purchases

Most people don't realize that a lot of limited edition shoes hold their value and even can increase their value depending on market trends, number of pairs, colorways, etc. I actually enjoy keeping up with as much about shoe pricing as I can, which can be used to my advantage when it comes to buying and selling my shoes. To start my story of justyfing a $600 pair of shoes, I'm going to start with two pairs of Jordan 1's that I managed to purchase for pretty good deals.

  • Reverse Shattered Backboard Jordan 1's: $160
  • Jordan 1 Bred's: $220

Selling Both Pairs

After wearing both pairs probably around 10 times each, keeping them in good shape, I sold them to an online sneaker shop, Direct Kicks. I was able to get good money for these shoes as the demand for Jordan 1's has increased over the last year.

  • Sold as a Package Deal: $575

Reinvesting

I wanted to use the money I made from selling the two Jordan 1's to invest in an even nicer pair of shoes. I had my eyes on the recent collaboration between Nike and designer brand Off-White. As part of their collaboration, Nike released 10 very exclusive run colorways of popular Nike shoes.

List of the 10 Off-White Nike Shoes

  1. Jordan 1
  2. Nike Hyperdunk
  3. Nike Vapormax
  4. Nike Air Max 97
  5. Converse All Star
  6. Nike Presto
  7. Nike Air Force 1
  8. Nike Air Max 90
  9. Nike Blazer
  10. Nike Zoom Fly

I decided to buy the Nike Air Force One Off-White's since their resell prices were sort of in the middle of the whole collections resell prices. They also seemed more durable than a lot of the other shoes from the collection and would look good regardless if I chose to wear them with pants or shorts. I watched several youtube reviews on the shoes before making my final decision.

Attached Video Review

Buying the Shoes

After watching prices on the Air Force 1's for about a week on a website called StockX, I finally purchased the shoes for $600. Paying this much for a pair of shoes will sound ridiculous most people including my parents. However, funny enough, the prices on these shoes have increased even more since I bought them. If you check today according to StockX the average price for a new pair of these shoes runs between $800-$882. Though I have not kept my pair new, as long as I keep them in good condition after wearing them, I will most likely be able to get more money out of selling them then I paid for them new, which completely justifies my $600 spent on the shoes.
